Lithium-ion batteries, commonly known as lithium batteries, are rechargeable power sources widely used in portable electronic devices such as smartphones, laptops, and electric vehicles. They are preferred for their high energy density, allowing them to store more energy in a smaller and lighter package compared to other battery types. Lithium batteries also exhibit low self-discharge rates, meaning they retain their charge when not in use for extended periods. However, they require careful handling and charging to prevent overheating, which can lead to safety hazards such as fires or explosions.
